Reading and Writing: Romanization

In the previous sections, we have explored letters, pronunciations, syllables, and combinations. Here, we will go over official romanization of each letter. Romanization is the way of writing Korean words using English letters.

For example, while “비” sounds like like “bee” to English-speakers, it is romanized as “bi” instead.

Keep in mind that while ㅙ (wae), ㅞ (we), and ㅚ(oe) are spelled differently in English romanization, they are pronounced the same in Korean!

The use and dependence on romanization for Korean-language beginners is discouraged because romanization can become confusing and unclear, such as the note about ㅙ, ㅞ and ㅚ above. Instead, try getting used to recognizing and reading korean letters as early as you can! This will especially help in the lessons later to come.

SPECIAL RULE (ᄉ): In the Alphabet page, we learned the consonant “ᄉ” that produces the “S” sound. However, there is a special rule that changes pronunciation that romanization does not show. When ᄉ starts a syllable and the vowel ㅣ or the “y-” vowels (ㅑ,ㅕ,ㅑ, ㅛ,ㅠ) follow, then ᄉ produces a “sh” sound instead.

Keep in mind that “sh” is not romanized, and only “s” is written instances when “sh” occurs.

Solidify your understanding of romanization by looking at the examples below:

  1. 신도림 Sin + do + rim = Sindorim (even though it’s pronounced “shindorim”)
  2. 회기 Hoe + gi = Hoegi
  3. 강남 Gang + nam = Gangnam
  4. 방탄소년단 bang + tan + so +nyeon + dan = Bangtansonyeondan
